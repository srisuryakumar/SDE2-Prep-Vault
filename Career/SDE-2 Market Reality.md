# The SDE-2 Market Reality

### What SDE-2 base salaries actually look like in India

Forget the headline numbers you see on LinkedIn ("I went from 8 LPA to 45 LPA in 6 months!"). Most of those are total compensation (TC) — base plus stock plus bonus — and a meaningful chunk are India offers from US-headquartered companies where stock is RSUs vesting over four years, not cash you get on day one. You need to think in **base salary** because base is what hits your account every month and what every subsequent offer gets benchmarked against.

Here's what the data actually shows for SDE-2 (sometimes called SE2, L4, P40, or "Senior Software Engineer I" depending on the company) in India, based on current public compensation data:

| Company | SDE-2 Total Comp Range (India) | Approx. Base Component | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Atlassian | ₹40L – ₹95L+ | ₹35L – ₹60L | Wide band by level (P30–P50); RSUs are a real, liquid component since it's publicly listed |
| Razorpay | ₹26L – ₹68L | ₹22L – ₹45L | Band varies hugely by when you joined and which BU; payments-domain depth pays a premium |
| PhonePe | ₹30L – ₹70L | ₹25L – ₹48L | UPI-scale systems experience is specifically rewarded here |
| Stripe (India) | ₹45L – ₹90L+ | ₹38L – ₹60L | Global pay philosophy, among the higher payers for India-based SDE-2 |
| Coinbase | ₹40L – ₹85L | ₹32L – ₹55L | Smaller India team, comp benchmarked closer to US bands |
| Rippling | ₹40L – ₹80L | ₹35L – ₹55L | Aggressive hiring, pays well to compete with bigger names |
| Swiggy | ₹24L – ₹45L | ₹20L – ₹35L | Useful as practice/leverage, not usually a ₹50L-base destination at SDE-2 |
| Meesho | ₹22L – ₹42L | ₹18L – ₹32L | Same — good interview reps, modest ceiling at this level |
| Groww | ₹25L – ₹50L | ₹20L – ₹38L | Improving bands as the company scales |
| CRED | ₹28L – ₹55L | ₹22L – ₹40L | Historically strong payer, has tightened bands in recent cycles |

A few honest caveats, because a book that oversells the number is useless to you:

- These are ranges, not promises. Where you land depends on your performance in the loop, your current comp (used as an anchor whether companies admit it or not), and how tightly you negotiate.
- ₹50 LPA **base** (not TC) is achievable at SDE-2 — but realistically you're talking about Atlassian, Stripe, Coinbase, Rippling, and similar global-pay-band companies, or you're talking about TC inclusive of stock at Razorpay/PhonePe/Groww at the top of their bands.
- These numbers move. Verify against Levels.fyi and Glassdoor before you anchor any real negotiation on a specific figure from this book.

### Why ₹50 LPA base is achievable but requires specific targeting

The mistake most engineers at ₹5 LPA make is applying broadly and hoping the market sorts them into the right bucket. It won't. Recruiting pipelines filter on signals — company pedigree, keyword match, referral status — long before anyone evaluates your actual skill. If you apply the same way to Swiggy and to Stripe, you'll get the same generic outcome: a coding screen, maybe, and then silence.

₹50 LPA base is a *targeting* problem, not a *talent* problem, for most engineers with 3 solid years of experience. The companies in the top half of the table above are not hiring for "anyone who can clear DSA." They're hiring for engineers who can be specific about systems they've built, who show up already speaking the company's domain language (payments, fintech infra, developer tooling), and who arrive through a channel that bypasses the generic resume pile.

### The companies that pay it

**Tier 1 — realistic ₹50L+ base targets:** Razorpay (top of band), PhonePe (top of band), Rippling, Atlassian, Stripe, Coinbase. These are your primary targets. Build your resume, your LinkedIn content, and your networking specifically toward these six.

**Tier 2 — practice and leverage:** Meesho, Swiggy, Groww, CRED. Don't dismiss these. Interviewing here, even if you don't intend to join, does three things: it sharpens your interview performance before you face a Tier 1 company, it gives you a real competing offer to use as negotiation leverage, and it builds your confidence that you can clear loops consistently rather than treating your dream company as a one-shot lottery ticket.

### The 4-scenario model

Plan your search expecting one of four outcomes, not just the best one:

- **Conservative (₹25–35 LPA):** You clear a Tier 2 company or land at the lower band of a Tier 1 company. Still roughly 5–7× your current base. A legitimate win if this is your first real search.
- **Expected (₹35–50 LPA):** You clear at least one Tier 1 company mid-band, possibly with a competing Tier 2 offer to negotiate against. This is the realistic target for a well-executed 4–6 month search.
- **Aggressive (₹50–65 LPA):** You clear two or more Tier 1 companies and run a real negotiation between them, or you land a strong band at Stripe/Atlassian/Coinbase. Achievable with strong DSA + system design performance and disciplined negotiation.
- **Best-case (₹65+ LPA):** Multiple competing Tier 1 offers, exceptional interview performance, and a negotiation that plays them against each other. Possible, not the default — treat it as upside, not a target you'll feel like a failure for missing.

Plan around the "expected" scenario. Get genuinely excited about "aggressive." Don't anchor your self-worth to "best-case" — a handful of people who post it on LinkedIn are not the base rate.

### Why referrals convert at 5–10× the rate of cold applications

A cold application at a Tier 1 company is competing against 200–800 other applications for the same opening, most filtered by an ATS before a human ever opens them. A referral routes you directly to a recruiter's queue, frequently with an internal note vouching for you. Internal data that companies have shared publicly over the years puts referral-to-interview conversion at roughly 5–10× cold application rates, and referral hires close faster and at marginally better offers on average, because the company has already de-risked the hire socially.
