120 days. ₹5 LPA → ₹[X] LPA. Here's what it actually took.

The honest version, not the highlight reel:

Day 1: Created two empty GitHub repos. Pushed a README. Called it a win.
Day 8: Could not solve Two Sum without looking it up.
Day 30: First Spring Boot API deployed. Felt like the hardest thing I'd done.
Day 45: Kafka consumer group worked for the first time. Ran around the room.
Day 60: Mock interview: 4.5/10. Partner said "you know the concepts but not the clock."
Day 75: Got a referral from a connection I'd made on LinkedIn 6 weeks earlier.
Day 90: Scalable E-Commerce Platform pushed to GitHub. 6 microservices. Actually works.
Day 103: First real SDE-2 interview. DSA round. Passed.
Day 114: System Design round. Rate Limiter + Notification System combo. Passed.
Day 120: Offer letter arrived. Signed.

What I spent the 120 days on:
→ 300+ LeetCode problems
→ 6 GitHub repositories, 700+ commits
→ 15 system designs, 10 LLD implementations
→ 34 LinkedIn posts, 150+ connections made
→ 8 mock interviews with my accountability partner
→ 200+ applications, 14 interview processes, 3 final rounds, 1 offer

The thing that surprised me most:
The projects did more work than the LeetCode scores.
Interviewers visited GitHub. They saw daily commits.
The E-Commerce platform became a 20-minute conversation in itself.

It is possible. It took 120 days.
