## SQL Practice
- Topic: JOINs and anti-joins
- Problem Statement: Given a `users` table and an `orders` table, find all users who have never placed an order.
- Solution:
  ```postgresql
  SELECT u.id, u.name
  FROM users u
  LEFT JOIN orders o ON u.id = o.user_id
  WHERE o.id IS NULL;
  ```
- Note: Anti-joins (using LEFT JOIN + IS NULL) are extremely common in OAs because they test your ability to find missing records efficiently compared to `NOT IN` subqueries.
