# Offer Management and Negotiation

## The First Offer Call
**Never accept on the first call.** You lose all negotiating leverage.
*Script:* "That's great to hear, thank you... I want to give this the consideration it deserves rather than respond on the spot. When do you need a decision by, and is it alright if I follow up with you in [X days] once I've had time to review everything?"

## Research Phase (Anchoring)
- Use **Levels.fyi**, **Glassdoor**, and **Blind**.
- Target the 75th percentile of the base pay for that level, not the median (the median includes people who didn't negotiate).

## The Counter-Offer Call
- **No Competing Offer:** Anchor on market data and role scope. "I was hoping we could look at a base closer to ₹46 LPA... I think that better reflects the scope of what I'd be taking on."
- **With Competing Offer:** "I have a competing offer with a base of ₹50 LPA. I'm genuinely more excited about the work here... is there flexibility to move closer to that number?" (Never lie about competing offers).
- **Below Your Minimum:** State your real minimum. Ask for creative structuring (signing bonus, early performance review) to bridge the gap.

## What Else to Negotiate
- **Joining Bonus:** Often the easiest lever to move to bridge a gap.
- **ESOPs:** Discount private company ESOPs heavily (50-80%) when comparing against cash.
- **Remote Flexibility / Extra PTO / Early Performance Review (e.g., 6 months).**

## Getting it in Writing
Do not resign from your current job until you have a signed offer letter detailing all negotiated terms. Verbal promises do not count.
