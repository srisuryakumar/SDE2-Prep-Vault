# LinkedIn Content Strategy

## Why Post Content?
Posting content (e.g., 2 times a week) establishes a public record of your learning and technical depth. It transitions you from being a passive consumer to an active participant in the engineering community, which attracts peers, recruiters, and hiring managers.

## Content Phases
1. **Weeks 1-4: Establish the Learning Journey**
   - Be authentic about where you are (e.g., "I know Spring Boot, but I'm shaky on system design").
   - Share simple "ah-ha" moments (e.g., caching vs. fixing an N+1 query).
2. **Weeks 5-8: Demonstrate Technical Depth**
   - Post deep dives on specific trade-offs (e.g., Cache-aside vs Write-through, Composite index ordering).
   - Use diagrams (flowcharts, architecture sketches) to explain complex topics like Event-driven architecture or CAP theorem.
3. **Weeks 9-12: Show Project Achievements & System Thinking**
   - Build a real project (like a rate limiter) and post about the design decisions, not just the code.
   - Share metrics (e.g., "p99 added latency of 4.2ms"). Talk about bugs you found under load.
4. **Weeks 13-17: Signal Interview Readiness**
   - Clearly announce you are looking for roles.
   - Share your interview frameworks and resources.
   - Be honest about failures (e.g., bombing a mock interview), as vulnerability builds trust and connection.

## Post Formats That Work
- **Text Stories:** Good for authentic reflections and debugging war stories.
- **Polls:** Great for reach and sparking debate (e.g., SQL vs NoSQL).
- **Carousels (PDFs):** Highly bookmarkable. Good for "Top 5 resources" or "5 mistakes I made."
- **Diagrams:** Clean visual explanations of system architectures get saved and shared widely.
