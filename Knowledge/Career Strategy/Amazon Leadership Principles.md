# Amazon Leadership Principles

Amazon interviewers are assigned 2-3 of the 16 Leadership Principles to probe specifically. They rate you independently against each. You can pass the technical rounds and still get rejected if your LP coverage is weak.
*Use your STAR stories to map to these principles.*

## The 16 Leadership Principles (What They Actually Test)
1. **Customer Obsession:** Starting from the customer's problem, not internal convenience. (e.g. Story 15 - Customer-facing incident communication)
2. **Ownership:** Acting like an owner beyond formal scope. (e.g. Story 14 - Cross-team escalation matrix)
3. **Invent and Simplify:** Finding genuinely simpler solutions. (e.g. Story 1 - Re-designing reconciliation logic)
4. **Are Right, A Lot:** Good judgment balanced with seeking evidence. Willingness to be wrong. (e.g. Story 19 - Changing mind on a rewrite)
5. **Learn and Be Curious:** Targeted, self-directed learning applied to real problems. (e.g. Story 7 - Learning Kafka Streams fast)
6. **Hire and Develop the Best:** Helping others grow. (e.g. Story 9 - Helping junior teammate find their voice)
7. **Insist on the Highest Standards:** Pushing for quality when no one is watching. (e.g. Story 6 - Finding float precision bug)
8. **Think Big:** Proposing solutions beyond the minimum viable fix. (e.g. Story 17 - Webhook dashboard for partners)
9. **Bias for Action:** Fast, reversible decisions with sound reasoning. (e.g. Story 5 - Feature-flagging a broken dependency)
10. **Frugality:** Resourcefulness (more with less). (e.g. Story 8 - Slack bot instead of heavy process)
11. **Earn Trust:** Candor, especially with bad news. (e.g. Story 18 - Communicating missed deadline early)
12. **Dive Deep:** Willingness to get into the data/details personally. (e.g. Story 16 - Benchmarking service-mesh latency)
13. **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit:** Disagreeing respectfully, then committing fully to the decision. (e.g. Story 3 - Validation layer disagreement)
14. **Deliver Results:** Pushing through obstacles to ship quantified outcomes. (e.g. Story 4 - Pre-sale critical bug fix)
15. **Strive to be Earth's Best Employer:** Improving the day-to-day for colleagues. (e.g. Story 12 - Rewriting onboarding docs)
16. **Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility:** Considering broader impacts (privacy, security) of what you build. (e.g. Story 11 - Caching vs Consistency)
