# The Psychological Game

## Imposter Syndrome
It is normal but statistically wrong. It's a reaction to uncertainty and evaluation.
**How to counter:** Use evidence, not a pep talk. Re-read your STAR stories before an interview to remind yourself of specific, concrete things you have actually built and fixed.

## Rejection as Data
Treat rejections as data points. If you get 3 rejections citing "weak system design," you have a fixable problem. If you get 3 rejections with no clear pattern, that is normal statistical variance. Don't take it personally.

## Burnout Prevention
- **The Non-negotiable Day Off:** Take one full day off per week from *everything* related to prep/job searching. This is a structural safeguard for your judgment and performance, not a reward.
- **The Comparison Trap:** People only post their highlights on LinkedIn (survivorship bias). Compare your progress against your own baseline, not against someone else's edited summary.

## The Recovery Protocol
When you fall behind your plan:
1. Diagnose honestly (was the timeline unrealistic?).
2. Adjust the plan (extend the timeline, don't cram).
3. Restart from the *current* week, do not try to "catch up" on backlog and burn yourself out.
