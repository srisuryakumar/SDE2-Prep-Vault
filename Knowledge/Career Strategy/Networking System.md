# Networking System

## The Networking Mindset
60-70% of roles are filled before they're posted publicly. Treat networking as your primary channel.
**Rule #1:** Give before asking. Never open a cold DM with "Can you refer me?" Lead with genuine curiosity.

## The Weekly Ritual
- **Monday (Identify):** Find 10-15 people at target companies (engineers, recruiters, alumni).
- **Tuesday (Connect):** Send connection requests with short, personalized notes.
- **Wednesday (Engage):** Comment on 5-8 posts from your target network. No asks.
- **Thursday (Follow-up):** Respond to accepted connections.
- **Friday (Alumni):** Reach out to college/past-company alumni (highest conversion rate).

## Connection Request Templates
- *Engineer:* "Saw you work on [team]. I work in a similar space and would love to connect and learn how your team approaches [specific tech challenge]."
- *Alumni:* "Fellow [College] alum here. Saw you're at [Company] — would love to connect and hear about your path there."

## The Referral Ask (The Golden Rule)
Only ask *after* a genuine interaction (comment thread, DM chat, call).
*Template:* "Hey [Name], really appreciated our chat. I saw [Company] has an opening for [Role] and think it could be a strong fit given [specific reason]. Would you be comfortable referring me? Totally understand if not, no pressure either way."
*(The "no pressure" line is crucial—it removes the social cost of saying no, making them more likely to say yes.)*

## Networking CRM
Use a Google Sheet to track: Name, Company, Connection Type, Date Connected, Last Interaction, Next Action, Referral Status. This prevents warm relationships from going cold due to neglect.
