# Behavioral Interviews: STAR Stories

The STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is the gold standard for behavioral interviews. You need a bank of specific, concrete stories. Do not use vague generalizations. First-person ownership ("I did X", not "We did X") is crucial.

## Key Story Categories to Prepare

1. **Most Complex Technical Problem Solved:** Focus on bottlenecks and how you identified them (e.g., moving reconciliation logic from app to DB to reduce a 6-hour job to 50 mins).
2. **Production Incident Caused and Fixed:** Don't hide the mistake. Focus on how you found the root cause, applied a fast mitigation, then a proper fix, and what process you changed so it doesn't happen again.
3. **Disagreed with Tech Lead:** Show how you used data and written documentation to make your case respectfully, rather than arguing in a meeting.
4. **Delivered Under Extreme Time Pressure:** Highlight aggressive scope reduction. What did you *cut* or simplify to hit the deadline safely?
5. **Decision Made with Incomplete Information:** Focus on finding a "safe degraded path" (e.g., feature flagging) rather than just choosing to wait or proceed blindly.
6. **Found a Bug Nobody Else Noticed:** Shows attention to detail beyond your immediate assigned tickets.
7. **Process Improvement Nobody Asked For:** A massive SDE-2 signal. (e.g., automating a 6-step manual deploy process).

*Prepare your own experiences fitting these molds. Write them down in bullet points so you can recall them under pressure.*
