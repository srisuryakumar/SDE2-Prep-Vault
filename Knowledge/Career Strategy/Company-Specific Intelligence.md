# Company-Specific Intelligence

## Why Research Matters
Candidates who know the company's product, technical decisions, and domain convert at double the rate of those who prep generically. True research grounds your answers and "why this company" in specifics, rather than generic flattery.

## The Company Dossier
Before an interview, build a 1-page dossier covering:
- **Product & Domain:** What they build, who uses it, core technical challenges (e.g., payments consistency vs social media scale).
- **Recent Technical Moves:** Read their engineering blog. Look for outage postmortems or migration stories.
- **Interview Format:** Known rounds, quirks (e.g., bar-raisers, take-homes).
- **Culture Signals:** Leadership principles, on-call load, decision-making style.
- **Your Honest "Why":** Find 2-3 specific, true reasons you want to work there (e.g., "I saw you migrated to an event-driven architecture, and I want reps on that").

## Where to Find Intelligence
- **Glassdoor:** Good for interview formats and high-level comp.
- **LeetCode Discuss:** Search company tags for recent DSA questions.
- **Blind:** Unfiltered culture, comp, and on-call load discussions.

## 5 Questions to Ask the Interviewer (Signaling SDE-2)
Ask questions that show you think about sustainable engineering and process:
1. "How does the team decide between paying down tech debt and shipping features?"
2. "What does on-call actually look like day to day?"
3. "Walk me through how a significant architecture decision got made recently."
4. "What's something the team believed six months ago that's changed since?"
5. "What does a strong first six months look like for this role?"
*(Never ask generic questions like "What's the culture like?")*
